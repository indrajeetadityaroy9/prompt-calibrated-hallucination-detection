# Product Guidelines: AG-SAR

## 1. Documentation & Tone
- **Academic Rigor:** All documentation, public communications, and internal notes should maintain an academic and rigorous tone.
- **Precision:** Prioritize mathematical precision, correct terminology, and formal proofs where applicable.
- **Citations:** Proper attribution and citation of relevant research are mandatory.

## 2. Engineering Standards
- **Research-Grade Code:** Flexibility is prioritized to facilitate rapid experimentation.
- **Expectations:** Code must be functional and logically sound, but strict enterprise-level standards (e.g., 100% test coverage, rigid linting) are relaxed for experimental branches to maintain velocity.

## 3. Reproducibility
- **Strict Reproducibility:** This is a non-negotiable pillar of the project.
- **Requirements:**
  - All experiments must be executable via scripts.
  - Random seeds must be fixed and documented.
  - Environment configurations (dependencies, hardware assumptions) must be explicitly captured to ensure results can be replicated exactly.

## 4. Visual Identity
- **Publication-Ready:** All generated charts and visualizations must meet high-quality academic publishing standards.
- **Style:**
  - High DPI output.
  - Serif fonts (matching LaTeX style).
  - Minimalist, colorblind-friendly color palettes.
  - Clear, legible labels and legends.

## 5. Open Source & Community
- **Status:** Guidelines for community interaction (issues, PRs) are currently undefined and not a priority for this phase.
